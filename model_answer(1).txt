What is Photosynthesis?
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae and some bacteria convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen using sunlight. It occurs in the chloroplasts where the green pigment chlorophyll captures light energy. Photosynthesis is very important because it helps plants make their own food and also releases oxygen into the air. This oxygen is necessary for humans and other living organisms to breathe. Therefore, photosynthesis plays a key role in supporting life on earth.

What does the term “invasive” mean in the article, and why is it debated when describing reptiles like pythons?
In the article, the word “invasive” refers to species that enter a new environment, spread quickly, and may harm native plants and animals. Many biologists believe that invasive species can threaten biodiversity and damage ecosystems. For example, pythons in Florida are often described as invasive because they have spread and may affect local wildlife.
However, some researchers argue that the danger of invasive reptiles may be overestimated. They believe that people sometimes judge these animals based on fear rather than scientific evidence. Because of this debate, the term “invasive” can be controversial when used to describe reptiles such as pythons.
